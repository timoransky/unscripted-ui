Keep scrolling — the section header docks, then lifts.

The bar sits flat in the flow of the page. The moment it pins to the top edge it gains a shadow, separating it from the content sliding underneath.

Section title stuck: top

Here container-type: scroll-state lives on the sticky element itself, and a query asks whether it's currently stuck.

Scroll-state queries can only style the container's descendants, so the shadow is painted on an inner bar rather than the <header> node.

It's the classic "flat until it sticks, then it elevates" affordance — the one that normally needs an IntersectionObserver sentinel to detect the pinned state.

No sentinel, no observer, no scroll handler. The browser tracks stickiness and flips the query for you.

Scroll back to the top and the shadow fades out as the header rejoins the flow.

Because it's a real sticky element, the header keeps its landmark, its links, and its place in the tab order the entire time.
