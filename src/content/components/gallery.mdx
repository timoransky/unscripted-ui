## How it works

- Each thumbnail is a `<button command="show-modal" commandfor="gal-photo-1">` and each enlarged
  view its own `<dialog id="gal-photo-1" closedby="any">`; the invoker commands
  (`command="show-modal"` and `command="close"`) open and close it with no script. The modal
  `<dialog>` gives you the top layer, an inert background, focus trapping and Esc-to-close, and
  `closedby="any"` adds click-the-backdrop-to-close.
- The panel rests dead-center: `fixed top-1/2 left-1/2` with `[translate:-50%_-50%]`, sized
  `w-[min(90vw,40rem)]`, at `scale-[0.22]` when closed and `open:scale-100` when open.
- **The origin comes from `anchor()` on the animation frames.** `[anchor-name:--gal-1]` names the
  button and `[position-anchor:--gal-1]` on the dialog binds to it. Two frames pin to the
  thumbnail instead of the center: the `@starting-style` entry frame
  (`supports-[anchor-name:--a]:starting:open:[left:anchor(--gal-1_center)]`) and the closed frame
  (`supports-[anchor-name:--a]:[&:not([open])]:[left:anchor(--gal-1_center)]`), each with a
  matching `top`. So the panel grows *out of* the thumbnail on open and back *into* it on close,
  while the open state stays centered.
- `transition-[left,top,scale,opacity,overlay,display]` animates the travel, and
  `transition-discrete` (`transition-behavior: allow-discrete`) keeps `display` and `overlay`
  alive so the close animates too. The `::backdrop` fades with it from `backdrop:bg-black/0` to
  `open:backdrop:bg-black/60` + `open:backdrop:backdrop-blur-xs`; the explicit transparent base
  is what lets it fade out instead of snapping.
- The tile edge is an `outline`, not a `border`: `outline-1 -outline-offset-1
  outline-foreground/10` draws a hairline one pixel *inside* the tile, so it sits over the image
  and flips with the theme token. On hover the image zooms (`group-hover:scale-105`); that
  `transform` gives the image a stacking context that would paint over the outline, so
  `relative -z-10` keeps it beneath. The button itself is `isolate`, so that negative z-index
  resolves against the tile's own stacking context instead of dropping the image behind the
  grid. `will-change-transform` puts the image on its compositor layer up front, so the first
  hover frame doesn't re-rasterize and shift it a pixel.

## Accessibility

- A modal `<dialog>` makes the rest of the page **inert** while it's open. Clicks, focus and Tab
  can't reach the grid behind it. It traps focus inside and restores it to the thumbnail on
  close. That's the browser's native modal behavior, no focus-trap library.
- `<dialog>` supplies no accessible name, so `aria-labelledby` points at the caption. Each trigger
  wraps an `<img>`, so its name comes from the `alt` and no `aria-label` is needed. The icon-only
  close button takes an `aria-label` and its SVG is `aria-hidden`.

## The fallback story

- **Without invoker commands** (`command` / `commandfor`) the triggers do nothing. Until
  Baseline reaches your audience, open the dialog with a three-line shim or from your framework.
- **Without `closedby`** Esc still closes it (the platform default), but clicking the backdrop does not.
- **Without `anchor()`** the entry and closed frames fall back to the centered resting place, so
  it scales up from the center instead of travelling to the thumbnail: a clean centered lightbox,
  minus the origin. That is why the anchored position sits behind a `supports-[anchor-name:--a]:`
  guard on the transition frames only.
- **Without discrete transitions** the view appears and disappears instantly; you lose the grow,
  nothing else.

The technique has two limits. It animates layout position on a top-layer element, which is
leading-edge and differs across engines; where `anchor()` is unsupported it degrades to a
centered zoom. And it is not a pixel-exact morph: a separate dialog travels from the thumbnail's
position, not the thumbnail's own pixels. A true shared-element morph needs the View Transition
API (`document.startViewTransition`, JavaScript), out of scope for a zero-JS fragment.
