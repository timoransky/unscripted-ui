## How it works

- Each thumbnail is a `<button command="show-modal" commandfor="gal-photo-1">` and each
  enlarged view is a `<dialog id="gal-photo-1" closedby="any">`. Invoker commands open and
  close it with no script — `command="show-modal"` opens the modal and `command="close"` on the
  ✕ closes it. A modal `<dialog>` gives the top layer, a background that's inert to clicks and
  focus, focus trapping and Esc-to-close for free; `closedby="any"` adds click-the-backdrop-to-close.
- The dialog rests **dead-center**: `fixed top-1/2 left-1/2` with `[translate:-50%_-50%]`, sized
  `w-[min(90vw,40rem)]`, scaled down to `scale-[0.22]` when closed and up to `open:scale-100` when open.
- **The origin trick is `anchor()` on the transition frames.** `[anchor-name:--gal-1]` on the
  button names it; `[position-anchor:--gal-1]` on the dialog binds it.
  `supports-[anchor-name:--a]:starting:open:[left:anchor(--gal-1_center)]` pins the
  `@starting-style` entry frame to the thumbnail, and
  `supports-[anchor-name:--a]:[&:not([open])]:[left:anchor(--gal-1_center)]` pins the closed
  frame to it too (with the matching `top`). So it grows *out of* the thumbnail on open and
  collapses back *into* it on close. Only these transition frames are anchored — the open
  resting state stays dead-center.
- `transition-[left,top,scale,opacity,overlay,display]` animates the travel, and
  `transition-discrete` (`transition-behavior: allow-discrete`) keeps `display` and `overlay`
  alive so it animates closed too. The `::backdrop` fades in and out with the panel:
  `open:backdrop:bg-black/60` + `open:backdrop:backdrop-blur-xs` transitioning from
  `backdrop:bg-black/0` via its own `backdrop:transition-all` + `backdrop:duration-300` — the
  explicit transparent base is what lets it fade out cleanly instead of snapping.

## Accessibility

- A modal `<dialog>` makes the rest of the page **inert** while it's open — clicks, focus and
  Tab can't reach the grid behind it — traps focus inside, and restores it to the thumbnail on
  close. That's the browser's native modal behavior, no focus-trap library.
- `<dialog>` supplies no accessible name, so `aria-labelledby` points at the caption. The
  trigger gets its name from `aria-label` — with a real `<img>` inside the button, drop the
  label and let the `alt` name it. The close icon is labelled and its SVG is `aria-hidden`.

## The fallback story

- **Without invoker commands** (`command` / `commandfor`) the triggers do nothing. Until
  Baseline reaches your audience, open the dialog with a three-line shim or from your framework.
- **Without `closedby`** Esc still closes it (the platform default); clicking the backdrop does not.
- **Without `anchor()`** the closed and entry frames fall back to the dialog's centered resting
  place, so it scales up from the center and back down instead of travelling to the thumbnail. A
  clean centered lightbox, minus the origin — which is why the anchored position sits behind a
  `supports-[anchor-name:--a]:` guard on the transition frames only.
- **Without discrete transitions** the view appears and disappears instantly. Nothing breaks;
  you lose the grow.

The panel grows *out of* the thumbnail on open and collapses back *into* it on close, resting
dead-center in between — driven entirely by `anchor()` on the entry and closed frames, no
script. Two honest limits: it animates layout position on a top-layer element, which is
leading-edge and can differ across engines (it degrades to a centered zoom where `anchor()` is
unsupported); and it is not a pixel-exact morph of the thumbnail's own pixels — a separate
dialog travels from the thumbnail's position. A true shared-element morph of the thumbnail
itself needs the View Transition API (`document.startViewTransition`, JavaScript), which is out
of scope for a zero-JS fragment.
