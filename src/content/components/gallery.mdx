## How it works

- **Open and close with no script.** Each thumbnail is a `<button command="show-modal"
  commandfor="gal-photo-1">` and each enlarged view is its own `<dialog id="gal-photo-1"
  closedby="any">`. The invoker commands do everything: `command="show-modal"` opens the modal
  and `command="close"` on the ✕ closes it. A modal `<dialog>` hands you the top layer, an inert
  background (clicks and focus can't reach it), focus trapping and Esc-to-close for free.
  `closedby="any"` adds click-the-backdrop-to-close.
- **It rests dead-center.** `fixed top-1/2 left-1/2` with `[translate:-50%_-50%]` centers the
  open panel, sized `w-[min(90vw,40rem)]`. It sits at `scale-[0.22]` when closed and
  `open:scale-100` when open.
- **The origin trick is `anchor()` on the animation frames.** `[anchor-name:--gal-1]` names the
  button and `[position-anchor:--gal-1]` on the dialog binds to it. Two frames then get pinned to
  the thumbnail instead of the center: the `@starting-style` entry frame via
  `supports-[anchor-name:--a]:starting:open:[left:anchor(--gal-1_center)]`, and the closed frame
  via `supports-[anchor-name:--a]:[&:not([open])]:[left:anchor(--gal-1_center)]`, each with its
  matching `top`. So the panel grows *out of* the thumbnail on open and collapses back *into* it
  on close, while the open resting state stays dead-center.
- **What drives the travel.** `transition-[left,top,scale,opacity,overlay,display]` animates the
  move, and `transition-discrete` (`transition-behavior: allow-discrete`) keeps `display` and
  `overlay` alive so the close animates too. The `::backdrop` fades with the panel:
  `open:backdrop:bg-black/60` and `open:backdrop:backdrop-blur-xs` transition up from
  `backdrop:bg-black/0` via `backdrop:transition-all` + `backdrop:duration-300`. That explicit
  transparent base is what lets it fade out cleanly instead of snapping.

## Accessibility

- A modal `<dialog>` makes the rest of the page **inert** while it's open. Clicks, focus and Tab
  can't reach the grid behind it. It traps focus inside and restores it to the thumbnail on
  close. That's the browser's native modal behavior, no focus-trap library.
- `<dialog>` supplies no accessible name, so `aria-labelledby` points at the caption. The trigger
  gets its name from `aria-label`. With a real `<img>` inside the button, drop the label and let
  the `alt` name it instead. The close icon is labelled and its SVG is `aria-hidden`.

## The fallback story

- **Without invoker commands** (`command` / `commandfor`) the triggers do nothing. Until
  Baseline reaches your audience, open the dialog with a three-line shim or from your framework.
- **Without `closedby`** Esc still closes it (the platform default), but clicking the backdrop does not.
- **Without `anchor()`** the entry and closed frames fall back to the dialog's centered resting
  place, so it scales up from the center and back down instead of travelling to the thumbnail.
  You get a clean centered lightbox, minus the origin. That's why the anchored position sits
  behind a `supports-[anchor-name:--a]:` guard on the transition frames only.
- **Without discrete transitions** the view appears and disappears instantly. Nothing breaks.
  You just lose the grow.

The whole effect is driven by `anchor()` on the entry and closed frames, with no script. Two
honest limits come with the technique. First, it animates layout position on a top-layer
element, which is leading-edge and can differ across engines (it degrades to a centered zoom
where `anchor()` is unsupported). Second, it is not a pixel-exact morph of the thumbnail's own
pixels: a separate dialog travels from the thumbnail's position. A true shared-element morph of
the thumbnail itself needs the View Transition API (`document.startViewTransition`, JavaScript),
which is out of scope for a zero-JS fragment.
