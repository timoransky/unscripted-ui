## How it works

- Three `sr-only` radios, three labels as triggers, three panels — all **siblings**, because
  Tailwind's `peer-checked/name:` needs the radio to precede what it styles.
- `flex flex-wrap` + `basis-full` on the panels puts triggers on one row and the active panel
  below, no wrapper divs needed.
- `peer-focus-visible/name:` draws the focus ring on the label when the (visually hidden)
  radio has keyboard focus.

## The honest accessibility trade-off

This announces as a **radio group**, not a `tablist` — because it *is* a radio group. Don't
bolt `role="tab"` onto it: mixing ARIA tab semantics with radio behavior confuses screen
readers more than either alone. Arrow keys move between options and Tab leaves the group,
which is exactly what ARIA tabs require — you're trading the "tab" announcement for zero JS.
If your accessibility review demands true `tablist` semantics, that's a legitimate reason to
reach for a JS implementation.

## When the panels hold heavy content

`hidden` removes inactive panels from the accessibility tree and find-in-page. If that matters,
consider separate pages — tabs hiding important content is usually an information-architecture
smell anyway.
